what's katakana?

Katakana is a syllabary. Each "letter" actually represents a syllable. For instance:

  • カ ka
  • タ ta
  • カ ka
  • ナ na

カタカナ

Katakana is fun to learn, because it is used mainly to write loanwords into Japanese. These days, the vast majority of loanwords into Japanese are from English.

So, with a little practice you can memorize the Katakana symbols, and then you can play the fun games of trying to find and decipher English loanwords stashed in Japanese text.

You'll be surprised just how common Katakana words are. Even if you don't know how to speak Japanese at all, you'll be able to decipher some words.

This site is here to help you do the memorizing, and to practice deciphering.
